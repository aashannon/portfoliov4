inTents

A UX case study creating a better way to plan and connect for camping enthusiasts.

Overview

The inTents app brings together campers for trip planning, packing, and connecting — all in one centralized tool. This case study documents the full design process from research to prototype.

Research & Discovery

I began with interviews and competitive analysis. Key findings revealed users often juggled multiple platforms to plan, pack, and communicate.

Key Finding 1

Campers used 3+ apps to coordinate trip plans and resources.

Key Finding 2

New campers struggled to find trusted information quickly.

Design Process

Initial sketches and wireframes helped shape functionality like trip creation, packing lists, and campsite search. I iterated with user feedback.

Final UI

The final design offers group messaging, checklists, and camp locator tools in an easy-to-use, mobile-first experience.

What I Learned

This project strengthened my ability to design for logistical coordination and adapt feedback into polished flows. The biggest challenge was balancing flexibility with simplicity.

Takeaway:
Build tools that simplify—not complicate—real-life coordination.
